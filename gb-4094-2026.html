China GB 4094 (2026 Amendment): Physical Controls Mandate for Vehicle Touchscreens

On July 1, 2026, the 2026 Amendment to GB 4094-2016 takes effect in China, requiring all vehicles sold in the country to include a minimum set of physical operating controls for critical human-machine interface functions. For the first time in automotive history, a major market is legislating against the growing industry trend toward pure-touchscreen interfaces.

This regulation doesn't just affect new vehicles produced for the Chinese market. It creates a massive aftermarket retrofit opportunity — estimated at over 28 billion RMB — for physical button panel manufacturers and integrators.

What is GB 4094?

GB 4094-2016 is the China National Standard for Operating Switches and Indicators in Passenger Cars and other Motor Vehicles. It specifies the location, color, shape, and function of all switches used to control vehicle systems. The standard has been in effect since 2017 but the 2026 Amendment fundamentally expands its requirements.

Core Requirement

The 2026 Amendment requires at least 10 critical functions to have dedicated physical controls, including (but not limited to):

  • Hazard warning lights (central location, red)
  • Headlamp control (auto, position, low beam, high beam)
  • Fog lamps (front and rear)
  • Front and rear wipers
  • Turn signals
  • Front/rear demist and defrost
  • Windshield wiper interval control
  • SOS emergency call (eCall)

The Impact on the Automotive Industry

For years, automakers — especially Chinese EV brands — have been aggressively reducing physical buttons in favor of touchscreen interfaces. The philosophy was "fewer buttons = cleaner interior = more screen real estate." GB 4094 forces a reversal of this trend.
